Additional anatomy and movement research:

- [Sled Dogs of Denali (NPS education PDF)](https://www.nps.gov/dena/learn/education/upload/Sled-Dogs-of-Denali.pdf): Denali dogs vary in color and ear carriage; functional traits include thick coats, fluffy tails, sturdy bodies, long legs, and large compact paws.
- [Why the Denali kennels have a breeding program (NPS)](https://www.nps.gov/dena/blogs/why-the-denali-national-park-and-preserve-kennels-have-a-breeding-program.htm): park freight dogs are substantially built for trail-breaking and heavy loads rather than standardized for show appearance.
- [Mushing Sled Dogs (NPS video, J. Tomsen)](https://www.nps.gov/media/video/view.htm?id=A1FEDF49-47FA-4314-B090-FDCD9C1AE990): observed coordinated, focused working gait through snow and forest. The video itself was not copied into the repository.
- [Genomic analysis of modern sled dog breeds (University of Alaska Fairbanks)](https://scholarworks.alaska.edu/uaf_grad_bio_sciences/333/): supports treating the Alaskan sled dog as a performance-selected population with varied ancestry, not as a uniform show breed.
