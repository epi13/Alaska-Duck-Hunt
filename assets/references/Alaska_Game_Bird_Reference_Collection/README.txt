ALASKA GAME-BIRD IMAGE COLLECTION
Created July 2026

CONTENTS
- 10 bird folders
- 7 requested category slots per bird: sitting, standing, flying, male, female, summer plumage, winter plumage
- 69 real photographs in category files
- 1 documented gap: Spruce Grouse / flying
- CATEGORY_INDEX.csv maps every category file to a source ID and explains classification limits.
- IMAGE_SOURCES_AND_LICENSES.csv provides source-page URLs, attribution, dates, licenses, and processing notes.

IMPORTANT INTERPRETATION NOTES
1. “Sitting” for ducks and geese includes resting or floating on water, because those birds are not normally described as sitting in the same posture as a terrestrial bird.
2. Some photographs are reused in more than one category when one image legitimately meets multiple requested traits.
3. Canada Goose, Snow Goose, Brant, and Sandhill Crane are sexually similar. Their “male” or “female” files are representative adults unless the source explicitly identifies sex; the category index states this.
4. Most listed species do not undergo the dramatic brown-to-white seasonal transformation seen in ptarmigan. For those species, “summer plumage” and “winter plumage” files are seasonal representatives based on date, setting, breeding/eclipse condition, or typical adult appearance—not claims of a diagnostic full seasonal plumage change.
5. Spruce Grouse flight: no clearly reusable true-flight photograph was located. The folder contains flying_NOT_FOUND.txt rather than a mislabeled bird or copyrighted stock photograph.

PROCESSING
Category copies were resized to a maximum of 1600 pixels on the long side and re-saved as JPEG. No subjects were generated, composited, removed, or added. Source-image metadata was stripped during export. The manifest records that changes were made.

This collection is an image-research package, not a statement about current hunting seasons, locations, permits, or bag limits.
