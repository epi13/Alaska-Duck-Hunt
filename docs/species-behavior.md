# Species Behavior Profiles

Verified 2026-07-20. These profiles are compact game interpretations of stable natural-history patterns; timing, speed, flock size, and scoring are fictional tuning values. They are not hunting advice or legal rules.

| Species | Start and feeding | Flush and flight | Grouping / special behavior |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Mallard | Water or marsh edge; swims, dabbles, tips, preens | Head-up alert, rapid near-vertical spring, direct flight | Singles, pairs, small mixed flocks |
| Northern Pintail | Shallow wetland, tundra pond, mudflat | Sudden elegant climb; fast high wheeling | Long neck and pointed tail stay readable |
| American Wigeon | Water, shoreline, wet grass; notable grazing | Immediate energetic launch; irregular turns | Small groups, often mixed with dabblers |
| Green-winged Teal | Shallow pond or dense marsh edge | Instant launch; fastest tight zigzags | Small, tightly spaced flocks |
| Greater Scaup | Open water; rafts and synchronized dives | Longer water patter; low fast direct flight | Compact rafts and flight groups |
| Common Eider | Marine water or rocky coast; repeated dives | Long heavy water run; powerful low line | Loose rafts; heaviest duck launch |
| Harlequin Duck | Rough coast or fast stream; active dives | Compact quick launch; follows shoreline | Pairs and small feeding groups |
| Common Goldeneye | Lake, river, or winter coast; frequent dives | Short water patter; very fast direct flight | Small/medium flocks, rapid cadence |
| Greater White-fronted Goose | Tundra wetland, flat, or field; grazes with sentries | Alarm spreads before staggered run; strong lines/Vs | Family groups; can circle and reland |
| Canada Goose | Grass, wetland edge, marsh, water | Threat/alert, heavy run, broad steady turns | Families and V formations |
| Snow Goose | Tundra/delta staging flats; dense grazing | Disturbance launches in waves | White and blue morphs; family groups |
| Brant | Lagoon, saltmarsh, mudflat; eelgrass feeding | Skittish compression then rapid low launch | Tight, less orderly coastal groups |
| Sandhill Crane | Tall meadow/marsh; slow walking and probing | Concealed → partial rise → upright bonus → running takeoff | Deep slow beats and long glides |
| Spruce Grouse | Forest floor/path/low branch; quiet pecking | Freeze, crouch, explosive short climb | Usually single; often lands and may return |
| Willow Ptarmigan | Tundra, willow, snow; buds and berries | Low explosive flap-and-glide | Summer/winter camouflage; settles nearby |
| Spectacled Eider | Tundra pond or coastal water | Eider-like water run and low marine flight | Protected identification bird; never awards target points |

## Natural-history references

Behavior and habitat summaries were checked against Cornell Lab of Ornithology species accounts for [Mallard](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Mallard/lifehistory), [Northern Pintail](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Northern_Pintail/overview), [American Wigeon](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/American_Wigeon/lifehistory), [Green-winged Teal](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Green-winged_Teal/lifehistory), [Greater Scaup](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Greater_Scaup/lifehistory), [Common Eider](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Common_Eider/lifehistory), [Harlequin Duck](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Harlequin_Duck/lifehistory), [Common Goldeneye](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Common_Goldeneye/lifehistory), [Greater White-fronted Goose](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Greater_White-fronted_Goose/lifehistory), [Canada Goose](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Canada_Goose/lifehistory), [Snow Goose](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Snow_Goose/lifehistory), [Brant](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Brant/lifehistory), [Sandhill Crane](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Sandhill_Crane/lifehistory), [Spruce Grouse](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Spruce_Grouse/lifehistory), and [Willow Ptarmigan](https://www.allaboutbirds.org/guide/Willow_Ptarmigan/lifehistory).

Regulatory/conservation status is kept separate and uses official sources. U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service identifies Spectacled Eider as federally threatened in its [Northern Alaska Field Office species list](https://www.fws.gov/office/northern-alaska-field-office/species) and initiated a new status review in 2026 ([91 FR 17810](https://www.fws.gov/federal-register-publication/endangered-and-threatened-wildlife-and-plants-initiation-5-year-120)). ADF&G describes conservative Sandhill Crane management on its [official species management page](https://www.adfg.alaska.gov/index.cfm?adfg=sandhillcrane.management). Always consult current [ADF&G hunting regulations](https://www.adfg.alaska.gov/index.cfm?adfg=wildliferegulations.hunting); emergency orders and local restrictions can supersede booklets.
